Graphic Design
Classwork

As a student currently enrolled in Mason Gross School of the Arts at Rutgers University, I am constantly met with different art assignments through a variety of mediums as per my curriculum. My design classes have tasked me with different assignments that have pushed the way I have thought about shape, typography, and simplicity.





Distinctive Voices Lecture Series

As a part of a lecture series that was being held in the pandemic, my teacher assigned me the task of developing a brand identity and animated social media graphics for the event.

Jodrell Bank Observatory

For one of my classes, I was assigned to create a new brand identity for the Jodrell Bank Observatory. In doing so, I deconstructed how the world heritage site looked, and based my design from an aerial view of the structure, and based the colors on the time of day most tourists give the area a visit, which generally is daytime. I branded my logo through interchangeable mediums, such as envelopes, letterheads, invitations, websites, and even water bottles!


Elements Project

For one of my assignments, we were assigned three elements on the Periodic Table, and were tasked to make an infographic-styled poster for each element, but to incorporate real chemistry facts into it. The elements I was assigned was Aluminum, Lithium, and Nickel. With these elements, I attempted to recreate a valence electron diagram with actual nickels and lithium batteries. However, feeling unsatisfied, I and went further, and edited my elements project to be a Lewis Dot Structure of the elements, while defining the melting and boiling points of each of them.
